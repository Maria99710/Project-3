Artwork Installation

Spring 2019
In this project, the purpose is to build an installation that applies the strategy of interaction. Our team uses the strategy of instrument and the key is in the touch and how the user uses the art piece to get an experience that changes based on what they touch. Our concept is Beauty knows no boundaries: beauty standards are an everyday event to many, and it involves many motions in the physical world. It is not simply a mental process that we do, such as thinking, but a process that involves our bodies, and it requires actions that we do. We used materials of iPad, keyboard, wood, mirror, and beauty accessories to build it.

Brainstorming and Inspiration

We wanted to use the synchronization of buttons to reveal the image of beauty standards. We will use cardboard to make the appearance of the dresser. According to the design drawing, we divided the main body of the dresser into four parts, separated by cardboard. We will install a lamp for each part. The switch of the lamp will be set at four fixed positions on the tabletop of the dresser. Each time the user puts a cosmetic in the corresponding position, the corresponding lamp will be turn on. Each light will illuminate the corresponding face sticker. They are activated by the user and then the viewer is transformed from the role of a user into an interactor (Huthanmo, 2004, p.2). After the user puts out the four cosmetics, the four lights will all be on and a face with different skin colors will be displayed.

First Draft

Among the sketches above, our team uses some basic material to build a prototype for the installation. We connected two iPads to the Bluetooth keyboard, in order to control two keyboards at the same time, we used wood stick and glue to make a connection between them. Then glue the wood stick with the beauty accessories(as the picture shows below) which means when the user press the beauty accessories, the face(picture) will be changed on the iPad.
